Summary

Key Takeaway: Deep work is becoming increasingly rare precisely as it becomes increasingly valuable in the modern economy.

Newport identifies three trends undermining deep work in the modern workplace:

  • Open offices -- constant interruptions and noise
    📌 Highlighted in Original
  • Instant messaging -- expectation of immediate response
  • Social media -- algorithmic attention capture

The concept of "attention residue" is central: when you switch between tasks, part of your cognitive capacity remains stuck on the previous task. This residue reduces performance on the current task. 📌 Note in Original

Newport argues that knowledge workers who cultivate the ability to perform deep work will thrive, while those who do not will fall behind as shallow tasks become increasingly automated.
