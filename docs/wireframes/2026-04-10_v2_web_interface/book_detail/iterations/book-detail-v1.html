KEY CONCEPTS
Deep Work Shallow Work Attention Residue Deliberate Practice Productive Meditation

Newport argues that deep work — professional activities performed in a state of distraction-free concentration — is becoming increasingly rare and increasingly valuable in our economy.

The core thesis is built on two observations:

  • The ability to perform deep work is becoming rare due to open offices, instant messaging, and social media
  • At the same time, deep work is becoming more valuable as our economy shifts toward complex information work
  • Those who cultivate this ability will thrive; those who don't will struggle

The book divides into two parts: The Idea (why deep work matters) and The Rules (four disciplines for cultivating it)...

KEY CONCEPTS
Open Floor Plans The Metric Black Hole Busyness as Proxy
  • Newport identifies three trends making deep work rare: open floor plans, instant messaging culture, and social media presence expectations
  • The Metric Black Hole: knowledge work lacks clear productivity metrics, so workers default to visible busyness
  • Busyness as a Proxy for Productivity: without better measures, doing lots of stuff in a visible manner becomes the default
  • The cult of the Internet: any technology that offers any benefit is embraced without cost-benefit analysis...

Key Design Decisions

  • Left panel: Book meta + 3 sub-tabs (Sections TOC, Concepts list, Notes/Annotations). Stays visible while reading.
  • Section TOC: Grouped by section_type (Chapters, Back Matter). Colored dot = eval status (green=pass, orange=partial, red=fail, gray=no eval). Active section highlighted.
  • Right panel tabs: Original | Summary | Eval — for each section. Book Summary is a special top-level item in the TOC.
  • Summary version selector: Dropdown showing preset + date. "History" link to see all versions and compare.
  • Action buttons: Summarize (opens preset picker), Evaluate, ⋯ menu (Edit metadata, Edit sections, Export, Delete, Re-import).
  • Prev/Next navigation: Quick section traversal without using the TOC.
  • Concepts as clickable chips: Click to see definition inline or navigate to concept detail.
  • Max-width 720px on content for comfortable reading line length.
